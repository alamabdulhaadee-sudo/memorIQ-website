# 01 — Brand

## Brand direction

**Bold Contemporary.** Think: confident design studio, contemporary fashion brand, editorial magazine from 2026 — not wedding magazine from 2015. The visual language signals that MEMORIQ is a step above the usual photo booth vendor without ever using the word "luxury."

## Brand personality

- **Confident, not cocky.** We know our work is excellent. We show it through evidence, not adjectives.
- **Current, not trendy.** We move with contemporary design language but we don't chase every trend.
- **Direct, not aggressive.** We say what we mean. We take a position. We don't soften everything into marketing mush.
- **Detail-obsessed.** Every design choice whispers: "If they care this much about their website, imagine their work at your event."

## Voice principles

### Do say
- Declarative statements that take a position ("Events are loud. Your memories should be louder.")
- Specific, concrete language ("DSLR optics," "instant prints," "the reception that doesn't feel like every other reception")
- Copy that differentiates — naming what competitors do badly is fair game ("better than an iPad on a stick")
- Sentences that end with confidence, not with hedging

### Never say
- "Capture the moment," "say cheese," "strike a pose," "fun props" — industry cliches, banned
- "Welcome to our website," "We are excited to," "Thank you for considering"
- "Premium" as an adjective (show it, don't claim it)
- Exclamation marks in headlines. One, sparingly, in body copy at most.
- "Click here," "Submit," "Learn more" — vague CTAs

### Voice examples

**Headline voice:**
> Events are loud. Your memories should be louder.

> We don't do "fun for all ages." We do specific.

> Evidence, not adjectives.

> Three ways to book us. No hidden math.

**Body copy voice:**
> A photo booth engineered for the moment. DSLR optics, studio lighting, instant prints — built for weddings, brands, and everything worth remembering across the GTA.

> June through October books 6–8 weeks out. If your date's open, don't sleep on it.

> Custom templates matched to your palette. A setup that looks like part of the design, not an afterthought near the bathroom.

## Emotional experience the website should create

Three feelings in sequence:

1. **"This looks premium."** Within 2 seconds of landing — visual design alone does this.
2. **"These people know what they're doing."** Within 15 seconds — social proof and portfolio.
3. **"I need to lock this in before my date's gone."** Within 60 seconds — urgency (structural, not fake) and a clear CTA.

## What to keep from the original strategy

- Brand name MEMORIQ (strong, unique, built-in meaning)
- Real reviews and real event photos as trust assets
- Live calendar as conversion tool (the biggest advantage vs. competitors)
- Focus on the GTA market
- Three event categories: Weddings, Corporate, Celebrations
- Transparent pricing, shown on the homepage

## What changed from the original strategy

- **Aesthetic direction:** was "refined editorial / wedding magazine," now "bold contemporary / design studio"
- **Color palette:** was charcoal + cream + champagne gold, now near-black + bone + oxblood-clay
- **Typography:** was Cormorant Garamond serif, now sans-serif display (see design system)
- **Tier naming:** was "Essential / Premium / Elite," now "Essential / Signature / Full Takeover"
- **Copy voice:** was polite and editorial, now direct and confident
